Option H — Action Center ⭐

Designed from actual fleet-app patterns (Fleetio, FleetTrack Pro redesign case study, Hicron Software design guide). Three deliberate moves:

Why this layout, in one paragraph: Fleet apps that work all do the same three things. (1) Service reminders bubble UP into a dedicated alert feed at the top — they don't live buried inside vehicle cards. (2) Critical alerts must look distinct from minor ones — the FleetTrack Pro redesign found that "alerts visually indistinguishable from minor warnings" was the #1 problem (sound familiar?). (3) Reduce columns: that redesign cut a 36-column vehicle table down to 10 with status chips and inline progress bars. The current page has 9 vertical sections per truck — same disease.

Why this is genuinely different from current page:
• Action Inbox at top — no more scanning every card to find what's broken. Click "Mark serviced" inline to clear.
• Severity is visual — urgent rows are tinted red, the broken truck is impossible to confuse with a healthy one.
• Rows not cards — you fit 4-8 trucks on one screen, not 1-2.
• Click a row to expand — the maintenance, service log, block-days form, photos all live in tabs inside an inline drawer (just like Fleetio). No sub-page navigation, no modal stack.
• 30-day timeline per row — see the next month's blocks without leaving the page.
• One-click actions on hover — edit/delete buttons appear only when needed, cutting visual noise (FleetTrack Pro redesign pattern).

Option D — All-info dense card ⭐

Everything from the current page without the wall-of-text feel. Each card has a fixed 5-zone layout: identity (color stripe + name + plate) → 4 health tiles (oil / sticker / days off / maintenance, each color-coded green/orange/red) → 30-day timeline (each square = a day; red = blocked-service, blue = personal, etc.) → maintenance list (color by status) → footer (service-history toggle + inline "block days"). The whole card has a left edge stripe in red/orange/green showing overall fleet health at a glance.
